A Timeline of Jonas Salk

  • 1914: Born in New York City
  • 1934: Earned a B.S. in Chemistry at City College New York
  • 1938: U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt founded the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis
  • 1941: Worked in the lab of Dr. Thomas Francis at the University of Michigan, leading to Salk's introduction to the world of Virology
  • 1947: Salk accepted an appointment to the Univesity of Pittsburgh School of Medicine
  • 1948: Undertook a project funded by the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis to determine the number of different types of polio virus
  • 1952: The worst outbreak of polio in United States history
  • 1955: Jonas Salk announces the polio vaccine being hailed as a "miracle worker"
  • 1957: 100 million doses of the polio vaccine were distributed throughout the United States
  • 1960: Founded the Salk Institute for Biological Studies, which today is a center for science and medical research
  • 1972: Published his first book titled "Man Unfolding"
  • 1983: Published his last book titled "Anatomy of Reality: Merging of Intuition and Reason"
  • 1985: U.S. President Ronald Raegan announced May 6 as Jonas Salk Day
  • 1995: Jonas Salk passes away as a man that saved thousand of lives

About Polio

Poliomyelits, commonly known as Polio is a highly infection viral disease mostly affected young children. The virus is transmitted by person-to-person spread mainly through the faecal-oral route or, less frequently, by a common vehicle (e.g. contaminated water or food) and multiplies in the intestine, from where it can invade the nervous system and can cause paralysis. Initial symptoms of polio include fever, fatigue, headache, vomiting, stiffness in the neck, and pain in the limbs. In a small proportion of cases, the disease causes paralysis, which is often permanent. There is no cure for polio, it can only be prevented by immunization. This information is from the World Health Organization, which is a great source of information about the disease.

More on Jonas Salk

Jonas Salk was a virologist that discovered and developed the polio vaccine. What was great about his life is that did not patent the vaccine because he considered it immoral to market a cure that could prevent pain to many families and people around the country and the world. Until 1955, polio was considered one of the most frightening health problems in the world. Salk help resolve this issue by developing the vaccine.
